3D Room Scanner & Object Mapper

Vision AI Approachable
Intermediate

Scan a room with a camera or depth sensor. The Jetson builds a full 3D model with accurate dimensions, identifies every object (couch, table, TV), and can generate a 2D floor plan blueprint. Then visualize how those objects would look placed in a different room.

How it works ‚Äî three approaches:

Option A: Phone Camera (cheapest)
  1. Record a video walking around the room with your phone
  2. Transfer video to Jetson (AirDrop, USB, network share)
  3. Jetson runs COLMAP or Gaussian Splatting ‚Äî extracts frames, matches features, builds 3D mesh
  4. YOLOv8 identifies objects in the scene
  5. Output: 3D model (.OBJ/.PLY) + object list with estimated dimensions
  Accuracy: ~1-2 inches. Good enough for furniture placement, not for construction.

Option B: Xbox Kinect (best value)
  1. Plug Kinect into laptop via USB
  2. Use libfreenect / Open3D to capture RGB + depth frames as you walk around
  3. Transfer point cloud data to Jetson
  4. Jetson builds mesh, detects objects, extracts measurements
  5. Output: 3D model with millimeter-accurate depth + floor plan
  Accuracy: ~5-10mm. Good enough for blueprints.

Option C: Depth Camera direct to Jetson (real-time)
  1. Plug Intel RealSense D435 into Jetson via USB
  2. Walk around ‚Äî Jetson builds 3D map in real-time (RTAB-Map SLAM)
  3. Live object detection as you scan
  4. Output: real-time 3D mesh updating as you move
  Accuracy: ~2-5mm. Best quality, fully live.

What You Can Get Out Of It

3D Model: Full textured mesh of the room. Open in Blender, rotate, zoom, measure anything.

Floor Plan Blueprint: Extract the floor plane from the 3D scan ‚Üí trace walls ‚Üí output a 2D floor plan with measurements in feet/inches. Like an architect's blueprint but auto-generated.

Object Inventory: List of every object detected: "Couch: 84" x 36" x 34" at position (3.2, 1.1, 0). Table: 48" x 24" x 30" at position (5.8, 2.4, 0)."

AR Placement: In a new room, overlay scanned objects on the camera feed to see how they'd look. "Would my couch fit in this corner?" ‚Äî see it in real-time before moving anything.

Gaussian Splat: The coolest output ‚Äî a photorealistic 3D capture you can fly through smoothly. Not just a mesh but actual captured light. Looks like a video you can move around inside.

The Catch (Being Honest)

Mirrors & glass: Depth sensors can't read reflective surfaces. Mirrors show up as holes in the scan. Cover them or scan around them.

Plain white walls: Photogrammetry (phone method) needs visual texture to match features. A completely blank white wall is hard to reconstruct. Depth sensors (Kinect/RealSense) don't have this problem ‚Äî they use infrared, not visual features.

Processing time: A full room scan takes 30-60 minutes to process into a mesh on the Jetson. Not instant. You scan in 5 min, then wait for processing.

Lighting: Phone photogrammetry needs decent lighting. Depth sensors work in the dark (infrared). Kinect actually works BETTER in dim rooms.

Large rooms: Scanning a whole house requires stitching multiple room scans. Each room works great. Combining them needs careful overlap between rooms (scan through doorways).

Accuracy expectations: You WILL get a usable 3D model and floor plan. It WILL be close. But don't expect architectural precision ‚Äî professional laser scanners ($50K+ devices) exist for a reason. For "will my couch fit in that room?" this is more than good enough.

Software Stack (all free & open source)

Scanning & 3D Reconstruction:
  - COLMAP ‚Äî photogrammetry from phone video/photos ‚Üí 3D point cloud
  - RTAB-Map ‚Äî real-time SLAM with depth camera. Best for live scanning on Jetson.
  - Nerfstudio ‚Äî NeRF-based 3D capture from video. Photorealistic results.
  - gsplat / 3D Gaussian Splatting ‚Äî newest tech, faster than NeRF, incredible quality
  - Open3D ‚Äî point cloud processing, mesh generation, visualization

Object Detection:
  - YOLOv8 ‚Äî identifies objects (couch, table, chair, TV) in real-time
  - Segment Anything (SAM) ‚Äî precise object boundaries for clean extraction
  - TensorRT ‚Äî NVIDIA optimizer, makes models 2-3x faster on Jetson

Floor Plan Generation:
  - Open3D ‚Äî extract horizontal plane (floor), trace wall boundaries
  - OpenCV ‚Äî convert 3D wall traces to 2D floor plan with dimensions
  - Matplotlib / CairoSVG ‚Äî render clean blueprint PDF/SVG output

Kinect Drivers:
  - libfreenect ‚Äî Kinect v1 driver (Linux, Mac, Windows)
  - libfreenect2 ‚Äî Kinect v2 driver
  - Open3D has built-in Kinect support ‚Äî easiest path

AR Visualization:

  - Three.js ‚Äî web-based 3D viewer (view scans in browser from any device)

MoveScan ‚Äî AI Moving Company Assistant

Product 3D Scan AI
Intermediate

A real product for moving companies. Customers scan their apartment with their phone before the move. Crew leads scan building routes (hallways, stairs, elevators, doorways) with a Kinect. The AI generates accurate inventory, dimensions, route clearance warnings, and quotes ‚Äî solving the biggest pain points in the moving business.

The Problems This Solves

Problem 1: "I just have a few things"
Customer says small move. Movers show up to a packed 3-bedroom. Wrong truck, wrong crew size, wrong quote. Company eats the cost difference or has an angry customer.

Problem 2: "It'll fit through the door"
84" couch doesn't fit through a 30" doorway. 45 minutes wasted trying angles. Sometimes you're hoisting through windows or disassembling. Could've planned ahead.

Problem 3: Stairs, elevators, tight corners
King mattress won't make the turn on the 3rd floor landing. Piano doesn't fit in the freight elevator. Crew finds out on move day when the clock is running.

Problem 4: Bad access / directions
"Easy to find" ‚Äî crew circles the block, can't find service entrance, doesn't know about the freight elevator, no idea which loading dock to use. 20 minutes burned before they even start.

Problem 5: Inaccurate quotes
Customer describes items over the phone. "A couch" could be a loveseat or an L-shaped sectional. Quote is wrong either way ‚Äî too low and you lose money, too high and you lose the job.

Indoor Drone Navigation with 3D Room Map

Vision SLAM Autonomous Hardware
Advanced

Scan your space with the Kinect to build a 3D map with room boundaries, furniture, and landmarks. Then the Tello flies inside that map ‚Äî it knows where walls are, where obstacles are, which room it's in, and where everything is. A web dashboard shows the drone moving through the 3D scan in real-time.

How It Works

Step 1: Kinect Scans the Space
  Walk through every room with the Kinect. Capture depth + RGB data.
  Jetson builds a full 3D mesh with room segmentation.

Step 2: Define Rooms & Landmarks
  The scan automatically identifies room boundaries by detecting walls and doorways.
  You can also place visual markers (ArUco tags, colored tape, printed QR codes) at key spots.
  Each room gets a label: "Living Room", "Kitchen", "Hallway", etc.
  Objects detected by YOLO get pinned to the map: "Couch at (3.2, 1.1)", "TV at (5.8, 2.4)"

Step 3: Tello Flies with Map Awareness
  Tello streams video to Jetson over WiFi (720p @ 30fps).
  Jetson matches what the Tello sees against the 3D map in real-time.
  Drone knows: "I'm in the living room, 4ft from the couch, 2ft above the floor, facing the TV."

Step 4: Live Web Dashboard
  Three.js renders the 3D room scan in your browser.
  A dot/icon shows the drone's position updating in real-time as it moves.
  Click anywhere on the map ‚Üí drone flies there autonomously.

Room Identification ‚Äî How the Drone Knows Where It Is

Method 1: Visual Landmark Matching (no markers needed)
  The Kinect scan captures what every room looks like from every angle.
  Tello camera feed ‚Üí Jetson runs feature matching (ORB/SuperPoint) against the scan data.
  Matches visual features to a known location on the map. Works like indoor GPS.
  Accuracy: ~6-12 inches. Good enough for room identification and rough positioning.

Method 2: ArUco Markers (precise positioning)
  Print small ArUco tags (like QR codes) and stick them on walls/doorframes.
  Each tag has a unique ID mapped to an exact (x, y, z) coordinate on your scan.
  When the Tello sees a tag, it instantly knows its exact position + orientation.
  Accuracy: ~1-2 inches. Fast, reliable, costs nothing (just print and tape).

Method 3: Room Geometry Fingerprinting
  Each room has a unique shape ‚Äî ceiling height, wall angles, doorway positions.
  Tello's downward camera + barometer + IMU gives rough positioning.
  Jetson compares visible geometry to the scan: "This ceiling height + wall distance = Kitchen."
  Accuracy: Room-level (knows which room, not exact spot). Great as a fallback.

Best approach: Combine all three.
  ArUco tags at doorways and corners for precise anchor points.
  Visual matching fills in between tags.
  Room geometry as a sanity check / fallback.
  Result: the drone always knows which room it's in and roughly where in that room.

Object Awareness on the Map

  During the Kinect scan, YOLO detects and labels every object:

  üìç Kitchen: Table (60"x36") at (1.5, 3.2) | Fridge at (0.2, 4.1) | Microwave at (2.8, 4.0)

Private Voice Assistant (Local Alexa)

AI 24/7 No Cloud
Beginner

Like Alexa/Siri but fully private ‚Äî runs entirely on the Jetson. Speech to text (Whisper) + LLM (gemma3) + text to speech (Piper). No recordings sent to Amazon. No subscription. Ask it questions, control smart home devices, set timers, play music ‚Äî all local.

Pipeline:
  1. Microphone picks up "Hey Jetson" (wake word detection)
  2. Whisper converts speech to text locally (~0.5s)
  3. gemma3:4b processes the query
  4. Piper TTS converts response to speech (~0.3s)
  5. Plays through speaker
  Total latency: ~2-3 seconds (comparable to Alexa)
